DPF Viewer Node — UI/UX Variants

Six directions to make the Ansys DPF viewer node more informative and quicker to operate. Each mockup keeps the existing node chrome and shows how the viewport, controls, and metadata could be reorganized.

02Timeline scrubber

for time-series results

Promote the playhead to a first-class scrubber beneath the viewport. Strip shows a heatmap of max-value per step, keyframe ticks, and a draggable playhead. Solves "no sense of total steps" and lets users jump directly to peaks.

03Side inspector (collapsible)

result browser + settings

A collapsible right panel replaces the "⋯ More" button with a proper result tree, variable component chips (X/Y/Z/eqv), legend min/max, deformation scale and opacity sliders. Keeps the viewport clean; everything stateful is in one place.

04Probe & pinned annotations

interactive inspection

Click the geometry to probe node/element values and pin them as floating labels. Left floating toolset switches between probe, measure, section plane, and isosurface. Great for comparing hotspots and writing reports.

05Floating HUD (chrome-less)

max viewport real estate

Strip out the fixed toolbar and footer. Controls collapse into a floating pill at top-center, metadata into a glass chip at bottom-left, mini-legend at bottom-right. Best for small node sizes where the current header eats 40 % of the height.

06Split comparison

A ↔ B · Δ mode

Two synchronised viewports for comparing load cases, timesteps, or solvers. Shared camera + shared legend range; a center sync pill toggles linked camera, linked playback, and a Δ mode that paints absolute difference.

Mockups are static HTML/CSS only — no JS. Variants 02 (timeline) and 03 (inspector) are the lowest-risk wins; 04 (probe) unlocks the biggest "aha" moment but needs a pick-ray backend from DPF; 06 (compare) is the largest architectural change but highest analyst value.
